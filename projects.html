Virtual Try-On: Offline Augmented Reality QA App

Date: May-Aug 2024


Purpose: Amazon Internship

Developed a native Android app using Kotlin and Java to record and replay AR sessions, significantly enhancing testing accuracy and efficiency, enabling team scalability.

Utilized OpenGL to render camera streams and assets within the AR experience.

Leveraged ARCore to manage AR session lifecycle, implement face detection, and extract essential data.

Eliminated dependency on physical cameras, enabling transition from manual to automated cloud-based testing.

Depth Estimation Model Comparison

Date: May 2025


Collaborator: Sajel Surati

Compared the performances of two depth estimation models, DepthPro vs. DepthAnythingV2, in underwater scenes.

Findings: both models perform excellently although they were never trained on underwater scenes. While authors of DepthPro claimed significant edge over DepthPro, we did not observe such a gap.

Smallest 3D Convex Hull Algorithm (C++, OpenGL)

Date: Fall 2024


Collaborator: Victoria Figueroa

Implemented the Gift Wrapping Algorithm to efficiently determine the smallest convex hull enclosing a given set of 3D points in O(nlgn).

Functioned for cases up to 1000 points in shapes of sphere, squares, and random.

Automated Profiler for Energy Dataset (Python)

Date: Jan-May 2024


Advisor: Sean Barker

Developed a Python program using HPC, Matplotlib, NumPy, and Pandas to generate automated “Energy Profiles” from HDF datasets.

Provided key metrics (longest/shortest activation, standard deviation) and visualizations (histograms) to understand device activation frequency, stability, and typical duration.

Empowered users by reducing 90% time required to analyze complex energy data in HDF format.
